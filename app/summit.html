Summit Electric Supply

I learned SO MUCH here. I had the opportunity to grow into this position. The team here was incredibly supportive and let me try a lot things, which resulted in me failing a lot, which resulted in me learning a lot.

I was originally hired to refactor legacy code and make updates to the customer facing website. After looking at the codebase and realizing just how daunting the task was, I convinced them to let me redesign the site from scratch.

Before starting with the redesign, I created a style guide to help us keep our code consistent and reusable, while maintaining a unified brand look.

The presentation of the site before the redesign had no direction. Most navigation items were hidden in a hamburger style menu, even on desktop, making it difficult for users to find what they were looking for. I wanted users to easily find what they were looking for, so a huge part of the redesign was making the navigation simple, bringing focus to what was really important.

Another important piece to the redesign was making it easier for users to shop and order online. Before the redesign, when a user went to look at products, they were placed on a page with 40,000 products and very limited filtering. I wanted to give customers the option to shop by category and intuitive filtering so they could find the products they wanted quickly and easily.

This led to the creation of the product landing page and the on page filtering options.

Besides the redesign, I learned all about Google Analytics in order to better utilize it for our benefit. I also invested in the performance of the redesigned site by running regular audits through Chrome Lighthouse and made fixes that increased our ranking in Google search.
